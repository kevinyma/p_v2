[Body]/[Armor]

Partner: Vinny Portonovo


A baseball catcher’s chest protector was used as a starting point to explore a number of architectural elements.

Deconstruction

According to its contruction, the object was systematically dissected.

Reassembly

The chest protector was reconstructed in a series of iterations. The body started to become a metaphor for site and habitation, as armor become a metaphor for architecture. The clearly defined hierarchical components of the armor (padding, foam, stitching, plastic covering) began to inform architechtonic elements as site, structure, superstructure and membrane.

Concept

What spaces exist between the body and the armor, and how does the nature of those spaces change when the armor recieves a force? These were questions I sought to explore throughout my iterations. I reconceptualized the armor as a series of metal tracks which accommodated undulating loops of wooden pieces.


Program

In the final phase of the project, a real site was identified. The components I had been working with were translated into a physical program space.
