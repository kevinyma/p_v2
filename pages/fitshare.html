Fitshare

As a semester long project for INFO 3450: Human Computer Interface, Boris Chan, Gabby Zhang and I proposed and iterated on solution around an existing problem. We created an online shopping tool for people aged 18-35.

Contextual Inquiry

The project began with extensive research on the subject: we interviewed users about their online shopping practices and observed them performing different tasks to identify user work practices.




Work practices were distilled into a design-informing diagram, which categorized user goals and frustrations. From this diagram, we determined that our product's design goals would be to reduce uncertainty about the physical nature of the product, and allow users to solicit instant, trusted feedback on their choices.




Candidate personas were established based upon the contextual information gathered from our real users of online shopping systems; candidate personas with similar goals were then merged, to create a primary and secondary persona.

Ideation

In the ideation phase, we brainstormed over 100 ways to address key issues that had come up during our contextual inquiry phase. We narrowed the ideas down and storyboarded the ones that fit our user personas the best.

Wireframing

We created a wireframe prototype which incorporated key features that addressed the goals of our personas. The full prototype can be viewed here.

Design



In the final phase, we designed a medium fidelity interface, and asked testers to “think aloud” as they performed a set list of tasks using our interface. The key issues that came up in these tasks were iterated upon.
