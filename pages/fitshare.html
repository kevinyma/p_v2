Fitshare

As a semester long project for INFO 3450: Human Computer Interface, Boris Chan, Gabby Zhang and I proposed and iterated on solution around an existing problem. We created an online shopping tool for people aged 18-35 who have experience with online shopping.

Contextual Inquiry

The project began with extensive research on the subject: we interviewed users about their online shopping practices and observed them performing different tasks to identify user goals. This allowed us to identify user goals, from which we crafted personas and began to formulate solutions to the problems that came up during the shopping process.
From this contextual inquiry, we decided our products design goals: to reduce uncertainty about the physical nature of the product, and allow users to solicit instant, trusted feedback on their choices.

Ideation

In the ideation phase, we brainstormed over 100 ways to address key issues that had come up during our contextual inquiries. We narrowed the ideas down and storyboarded the ones that fit best.

Wireframing

We created a paper prototype which incorporated key features that addressed the goals of our personas. The full paper prototype can be viewed here.

Design

In the final phase, we asked testers to “think aloud” as they performed different tasks using our interface.
